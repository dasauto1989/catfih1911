What is NGA Theory (Niggovian Generalized Approximation Theory)?

Niggovian Generalized Approximation Theory, also known as "NGA Theory", is the study of high-dimensional subject modeling using a decomposable state vector framework,
    allowing for complex human-like entities to be analyzed, quantified, and clustered across multiple behavioral and physical domains.

The Niggovian Property

The Niggovian Property defines a subject-space framework wherein each classified entity, most often referred to as a "State-Bound Form", is characterized by a
  12-dimensional rating vector, with each component reflecting a discrete behavioral or physiological trait.
  
  Each rating is assigned a weight, and the combined weighted vector determines both the classification tier of the State-Bound Form and the degree to which it expresses
  each associated trait state.

Example

Consider a particularly volatile State-Bound Form exhibiting advanced gluteal hypertrophy (colloquially referred to as a "double-globe configuration.)
  
       According to the Niggovian Property, this entity will map onto 12 trait states:
       1. Height
       2. Weight
       3. Body Fat Percentage
       4. Nasal Index

       6. Haircut Category
       7. Individual's Education Level
       8. Shoe Brand Affiliation
       9. Lexical Frequency of the "Term"
       10. Ease of Aggravation

       12. Area of Origin
